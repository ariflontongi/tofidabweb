Early Life and Career

Frank Dux was born in Toronto, Canada, but moved to California at a young age. He claims to have been trained in the martial arts by Senzo Tanaka, a supposed descendant of a long line of ninja warriors. Dux’s martial arts career is marked by his claim of winning the Kumite, an alleged secret martial arts tournament.

Controversies and Claims

Dux’s claims about his martial arts exploits have been a subject of controversy. Critics argue that there is no verifiable evidence to support his claims about the Kumite tournament or his training under Senzo Tanaka. Despite these controversies, Dux has managed to build a career around his martial arts expertise.

Frank Dux in Hollywood

Dux’s alleged martial arts exploits caught the attention of Hollywood, leading to the 1988 film “Bloodsport,” where Jean-Claude Van Damme portrayed Dux. The film was a commercial success and significantly boosted Dux’s fame and wealth.

Income from Fight Choreography

Following the success of “Bloodsport,” Dux worked as a fight choreographer in Hollywood. His expertise in martial arts made him a sought-after choreographer for action films, contributing significantly to his net worth.

Income from Books

Dux is also an author, having written books about his martial arts experiences. His books, such as “The Secret Man” and “Enter the Ninja,” have added to his income streams.

Income from Teaching Martial Arts

Dux has also earned income from teaching martial arts. He founded the Dux Ryu Ninjutsu school, where he imparts his knowledge of martial arts to students.

Legal Battles

Dux’s wealth has been impacted by several legal battles. He has been involved in lawsuits against magazines that have questioned his claims, as well as against fellow martial artists.
