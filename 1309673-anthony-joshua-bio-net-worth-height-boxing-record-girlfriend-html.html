Anthony Joshua early life

The young Joshua was born in Watford Hertfordshire England to Yeta and Robert Joshua. He spent his childhood days in a boarding school in Nigeria until Anthony Joshua parents, unfortunately, divorced shortly after twelve years birthday. Consequently, he had to relocate to the United Kingdom. He enrolled at the Kings Langley secondary school.

The famous boxer showed great interest and skills in soccer and athletics while growing up in Garston, Hertfordshire. He even reached the 100-meter high point in 11.6 seconds in his 9th year. Surprisingly, he decided to start boxing in 2007 at the age of 18 years. His cousin had suggested that he give it a try, which he did in due course.

Where is Anthony Joshua from in Nigeria?

Not everyone knows that Anthony is of Nigerian descent. The boxing champion belongs to the Yoruba ethnicity, which is one of the dominant tribes in Nigeria. Additionally, his hometown is in Sagamu.

This place is located in Ogun state, the western part of Nigeria. His mother, Yeta, is from this part of the country. Also, the famous boxer's father, who is half Irish and half of Nigeria, has an ancestral link to Sagamu.

Anthony Joshua record

In two consecutive times, 2009 and 2010, the 30 years old Anthony emerged as the winner of the Haringey Boxing Cup. When he was in his amateur stage, he triumphed in all the eighteen fights he involved in. Additionally, after defeating Cathal McMonagle and Eric Rechenlin in June 2011, he took the European Amateur Boxing Championship title, except for Romanian Mihai Nistor who later interrupted him.

After achieving a 40-3 record, in 2011, the Boxing Writers Club gave him the Amateur Boxer of the Year honour in the United Kingdom. Despite entering newly into the international scene, the first appearance that he made at the London Olympics in 2012 earned him a gold medal.
