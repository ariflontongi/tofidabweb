Bryson Tiller Daughter, Baby Mother (Friend), Age, Height, Bio

August 2024 · 4 minute read

Bryson Tiller is a talented R&B singer who burst onto the showbiz scene in 2015 after the release of his debut single, “Don’t”, which won him two BET Awards for Best New Artist and Best R Artist. & B / Male pop.

A native of Louisville, Kentucky, whose mayor gave the key to the city, subsequently naming March 12 Bryson Tiller Day in Lousiville, described his type of music as not typical R&B, but rather an amalgamation of trap and hip hop. Regardless, Tiller’s music has been well received by audiences, with “Don’t” notably garnering over 41 million views on YouTube.

Biography (Age)

Bryson Tiller was born Bryson Djuan Tiller on January 2, 1993, in Louisville, Kentucky. Details about his father are unknown, however, his mother died when he was just four years old. This left him and his younger brother Eric to be raised by their grandmother.

From an early age, Tiller was a big fan of music. However, it took him until he was 15 when he entered high school to start recording, singing and rapping. He attended Iroquois High School in southwest Louisville, Kentucky.

With life very fast on his hands, Tiller needed to support his family, so he quit school to find full-time employment with the franchise pizza restaurant Papa Johns, as well as the delivery company. of UPS packages. However, he still continued to make music from his living room with the hope of at least becoming a songwriter. He saw composer-turned-musician, The Dream, as his biggest influence at the time.

Bryson Tiller then moved to Miami to pursue his dream of becoming a singer, revealing that he had to sleep in his car most of the time. His life then took a turning point in October 2014 when he added ‘Don’t’ to his Soundcloud account.

Different labels and producers started taking notice of the track as it received huge shares online. This prompted him to officially release the song as the lead single from his debut album. In the meantime, music executives had begun demanding his signature. He ultimately opted to sign what was described as a creative partnership deal with RCA Records, turning down deals from labels such as Drake’s OVO Sound among many others.

Tiller had started gathering a lot of buzz as fans anticipated his album. The converted magazine Rolling Stone, in September 2015, featured him among its list of new artists people should know about. A month later, on October 2, 2015, Bryson Tiller released his debut album titled Trapsoul.

Upon its release, the album went straight to number 11 on the US Billboard 200 chart, peaking at number eight. The album’s second single, Exchange, was nominated for a Grammy Award and peaked at number 26 on the Billboard Hot 100, while the third single, “Sorry Not Sorry,” peaked at number 67.

More hits unveiled for the young superstarhe began to appear on many other artists’ records. He notably played in “Wild Thoughts” by Dj Khaled alongside Rihanna. He was also named to Forbes magazine’s 30, under 30 list of outstanding entrepreneurs, artists and celebrities under 30.

On May 26, 2017, Tiller released his second album titled ‘True to Self’. The album debuted at number 1 on the Billboard 200 and became the singer’s debut album.

Bryson Tiller baby mother (girlfriend), daughter

Bryson Tiller had a long term relationship with Markea Bivens who is also his baby mother. Little is known about Bivens as she and Tiller like to keep a low profile. Tiller, however, revealed that his hit song “Don’t” was inspired by his relationship with her.

It is unclear when exactly the couple started dating or if they are currently together despite numerous reports of their engagement. Regardless of the situation, the two work very closely and can be seen together as a co-parent to their young daughter. Images of their daughter, Harley, as well as her baby mother can be viewed on Tiller’s Instagram.
