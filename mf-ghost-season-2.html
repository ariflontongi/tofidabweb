Storyline & Plot

MF Ghost is a sequel to Initial D, and is set in the near future, where self-driving electric cars have replaced the traditional ones. However, in Japan, there is still a group of passionate racers who compete in the MFG, a street racing organization founded by Ryosuke Takahashi, the former leader of the RedSuns. The MFG is a prestigious and lucrative league, where the best drivers from all over the world gather to challenge each other in various courses and conditions.

The story follows Kanata Katagiri, a 19-year-old Japanese-British prodigy who has been trained by Takumi Fujiwara, the former downhill king and the driver of the legendary AE86. Kanata returns to Japan to participate in the MFG and to find his long-lost father, who is rumored to be the legendary racer known as MF Ghost. Kanata drives a Toyota 86, and proves his skills by beating many top-tier cars, such as the Lamborghini Huracán, the Ferrari 488, the Lotus Exige, the Alfa Romeo 4C, the Lexus LC500, and the Porsche 911.

However, Kanata also faces many rivals and obstacles, such as Ren Katagiri, his half-brother and the current champion of the MFG, who drives a Nissan GT-R Nismo; Shun Aiba, a former Formula One driver who drives a McLaren 720S; Ryo Takahashi, the son of Ryosuke Takahashi and the leader of the new RedSuns, who drives a Mazda RX-7; and many others. Kanata also meets many friends and allies, such as Ogata, his mechanic and mentor; Mika, his childhood friend and navigator; and many others.

As the story progresses, Kanata learns more about the history and secrets of the MFG, his father, and himself. He also develops his driving skills and style, and challenges himself to become the best racer in Japan and the world.
